# 30/60/90-day plan — GS Tax, Senior Associate, institutional clients

Purpose: hit the ground running on tax work while establishing a credible, visible position as the team's go-to for AI tooling — without over-extending and without becoming "the AI person" at the expense of "the tax person."

Guiding principle for every week: **tax deliverables first, AI leverage second, evangelism third.** Deliverables build trust; trust buys the room to teach.

---

## Days 1–30 — Listen, map, stabilize

### Primary objectives

1. Deliver assigned tax work at the expected Senior Associate standard. Full stop. Nothing below compromises this.
2. Build the end-to-end map of the GS Tax / institutional-client workflow.
3. Learn the AI stack as it actually exists at GS (not as I assume it).
4. Establish rules-of-engagement in writing before touching AI with anything sensitive.

### Week 1

- [ ] Standard onboarding: access, systems, team intros, compliance training.
- [ ] Read every piece of official documentation on the proprietary GPT and M365 Copilot.
- [ ] Identify the tax-technology / AI-enablement contact and schedule a 30-minute intro.
- [ ] Identify the compliance / privacy contact and confirm AI-usage policy in writing.
- [ ] Start `journal/` entries from day one.
- [ ] **Do not share Morgan Stanley anecdotes yet.** Listen first.

### Week 2

- [ ] Shadow at least one Senior Associate through a full workflow cycle.
- [ ] Ask each teammate: "What AI tool do you actually use, and for what?" — log answers.
- [ ] Pressure-test prop-GPT and Copilot on synthetic / public tasks to calibrate quality.
- [ ] Draft the first version of `conductor/tools.md` — turn assumptions into confirmed facts.
- [ ] First skip-level or manager check-in: confirm priorities and the quality bar.

### Week 3

- [ ] First substantive tax deliverable on my own (with senior review).
- [ ] Score all candidate workflows in `conductor/workflows.md`. Identify top 3.
- [ ] Start a private prompt file per top workflow — iterate on real (but fully generic) scaffolds.
- [ ] Join any internal Copilot / prop-GPT power-user channel.

### Week 4

- [ ] Second deliverable. Start the pattern of post-deliverable retros in the journal: *what would I delegate to AI next time, and how?*
- [ ] Document Morgan Stanley lessons **in generic form** (no MS-confidential material) inside `tools.md` for my own future reference.
- [ ] One-on-one with manager: share my 30-day observations and flag the AI workstream as something I want to run alongside core work. Get explicit OK.

### Day-30 artifacts

- `conductor/role.md` updated with real stakeholder names (local notes only, not committed to any shared repo).
- `conductor/tools.md` with "Confirmed" columns filled in.
- `conductor/workflows.md` top 3 identified with manager agreement.
- `journal/` has ~20 daily entries.
- Three private prompt drafts in `prompts/`.

## Days 31–60 — Build, demonstrate, document

### Primary objectives

1. Continue delivering tax work at or above expectation.
2. Turn the top-3 workflows into repeatable AI-accelerated patterns.
3. Publish my first internal prompt resource.
4. Propose one Copilot-leveraged ops workflow improvement with a measurable baseline.

### Week 5

- [ ] Third deliverable, demonstrably faster / higher-quality than Week 3's — with AI leverage called out explicitly in any retro.
- [ ] Prompt library v0.5 — refined, tested, with example inputs/outputs (synthetic only).
- [ ] Identify one workflow on the ops side (not my direct domain) that is a visible pain point and a good Copilot rollout candidate. Talk to one ops colleague about it.

### Week 6

- [ ] Prompt library v1.0 — share with one peer Senior Associate for feedback.
- [ ] Formalize my "what am I missing?" prop-GPT review pattern — document it.
- [ ] Begin drafting `tracks/copilot-rollout.md` — frame it as a proposal, not a plan I'm executing.
- [ ] Begin drafting `tracks/adoption-kpis.md`.

### Week 7

- [ ] Publish prompt library to the team (after review by the SA I trust most). Volume: ≥ 5 prompts, each with goal, system prompt, user-prompt template, example, failure modes, eval rubric.
- [ ] Circulate one Morgan Stanley lesson (generic, reframed as a question to the room, not a declaration): "At my last firm we saw X pattern — is that relevant here?"
- [ ] Schedule the Day-90 workshop. Title and abstract drafted.

### Week 8

- [ ] Fourth or fifth deliverable. Track time-saved and quality-delta in the journal.
- [ ] Draft of rollout framework shared with manager for feedback.
- [ ] First KPI proposal shared with manager.
- [ ] Teammates using my prompts: target ≥ 2 (organic, not pushed).

### Day-60 artifacts

- `prompts/` — five files, each complete with goal, system prompt, template, example, failure modes, eval rubric.
- `tracks/copilot-rollout.md` v1 — in manager's hands for feedback.
- `tracks/adoption-kpis.md` v1 — proposal posture.
- Concrete, journaled evidence of time-saved / quality-improved from my own workflows.

## Days 61–90 — Lead, measure, institutionalize

### Primary objectives

1. Deliver a workshop that earns real invites back.
2. Propose an adoption measurement approach to the right audience at the right level of ambition.
3. Institutionalize one reusable RAG-query pattern for prop-GPT that the team adopts.
4. Hand over something — genuine knowledge transfer, not just self-promotion.

### Week 9

- [ ] Finalize workshop material: `training/copilot-101-tax.md` and `training/proprietary-gpt-101-tax.md`. Short, demo-heavy, no hype.
- [ ] Dry-run the workshop with one trusted peer and one analyst. Collect structured feedback.
- [ ] Lock the KPI proposal — version presentable to an ED / VP audience.

### Week 10

- [ ] Deliver the workshop. Record feedback. Publish slides + prompt library link to the team channel.
- [ ] Follow-up 1:1s with anyone who raised specific questions.
- [ ] Submit KPI proposal up the chain (with manager's explicit sponsorship).

### Week 11

- [ ] Document my reusable RAG-query pattern for prop-GPT (the specific way I've learned to phrase queries for reliable, cited answers on tax-authority topics). File it under `prompts/` and `training/`.
- [ ] Pair with one analyst for an hour, walk them through the pattern.
- [ ] Manager 1:1: what's the next ambition? Am I taking on too much? Too little?

### Week 12

- [ ] Day-90 retrospective in the journal: what landed, what didn't, what surprised me.
- [ ] Version the framework itself: snapshot `conductor/`, `prompts/`, `tracks/`, `training/` and archive as v1.
- [ ] Set the next 90-day horizon: depth over breadth — one domain of tax I want to get demonstrably great at with AI leverage.

### Day-90 artifacts

- Workshop delivered, material filed in `training/`.
- KPI proposal submitted.
- Reusable RAG-query pattern documented and adopted by ≥ 1 teammate.
- Framework v1 archived.
- Explicit manager-level sign-off on the next 90-day horizon.

## Anti-goals — what I will not do in the first 90 days

- Bring up Morgan Stanley unprompted more than once per conversation.
- Advocate for new tools before I have mastered the existing ones.
- Propose a firm-wide rollout. My scope is my team.
- Publish anything externally (LinkedIn posts, conference talks) about AI at GS during year one.
- Let AI output reach a client deliverable without a full human pass.
- Use AI to simulate effort I did not actually put in.

> "I've spent the last 90 days learning the GS Tax workflow as a Senior Associate on institutional clients, delivering on my matters, and in parallel building a lightweight framework — a tax-flavored prompt library, a set of proposed adoption KPIs, and a rollout pattern we can use for Copilot on the ops side. The five workflows I've accelerated most are [X, Y, Z, …]. I'd like the next quarter to go deep in [domain] while extending the framework from my team to our wider group if you'd like to sponsor that."
