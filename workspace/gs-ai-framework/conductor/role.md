## Why this role, why now

Two simultaneous firm-wide rollouts make this an unusually high-leverage entry point:

1. A **proprietary GPT** built for internal use (expected: a RAG-grounded assistant over internal knowledge, policies, research).
2. **Microsoft 365 Copilot for operations** (expected: Outlook / Word / Excel / Teams / SharePoint, progressively extended).

Most new joiners will treat these as generic productivity tools. The differentiating move is to arrive with an explicit framework for (a) mastering them faster than peers, (b) translating them into tax-specific workflows, and (c) teaching others — which is what a Senior Associate is expected to do.

## Prior experience to carry in

- Copilot deployment and adoption work at Morgan Stanley — concrete evidence of what worked, what failed, what the change-management curve looked like, which user personas adopted fastest.
- Broader AI-agent experience — how to scope an agentic workflow, where RAG helps vs. hurts, how to evaluate outputs.
- Institutional-client instincts — what "good" looks like for deliverables, review cycles, and sign-off culture at a bulge-bracket firm.

## Success metrics — what "good" looks like

### Day 30

- [ ] Full map of the tax/institutional-client workflow (who does what, where data lives, what gets reviewed and by whom).
- [ ] Inventory of every AI tool I have access to — prop-GPT modes, Copilot surfaces, any third-party tools, any internal point solutions.
- [ ] Documented rules-of-engagement: what data can go where, what requires redaction, what is off-limits.
- [ ] 3 recurring tax tasks identified as highest-leverage candidates for AI acceleration (criteria: frequency × time-cost × error-cost × AI-suitability).
- [ ] First pass of my private prompt library started (≥5 prompts that I actually use weekly).

### Day 60

- [ ] Prompt library published internally (to my team or a relevant channel) — tax-flavored, reviewed by at least one senior reviewer.
- [ ] One measurable ops workflow proposed for Copilot migration with a rough before/after estimate.
- [ ] Shadowed at least one existing power-user; interviewed at least three colleagues on their AI habits.
- [ ] First workshop or lunch-and-learn scheduled.

### Day 90

- [ ] Workshop delivered; feedback captured.
- [ ] Proposed an adoption KPI set to my manager (see `tracks/adoption-kpis.md`).
- [ ] At least one prop-GPT RAG-query pattern documented and reusable across the team.
- [ ] Two or more teammates using my prompts unprompted (genuine pull, not pushed).
- [ ] Position established as *the person on the team to ask about AI tooling* — without being annoying about it.

## Non-goals

- Becoming the platform team. I am a power-user and enabler, not the engineer of prop-GPT or Copilot itself.
- Evangelizing AI for its own sake. Every use must earn its place on time-saved or quality-improved grounds.
- Shipping anything that touches client data without an explicit policy nod.

## Risks I am watching

- **Policy drift** — acting on assumed rules instead of checked ones.
- **Tool fragmentation** — prop-GPT vs. Copilot vs. third-party creating personal-workflow chaos.
- **Over-automation** — AI outputs entering client deliverables without a tax-professional check.
- **Perception risk** — looking like "the AI person" instead of "the tax Senior Associate who happens to be excellent with AI."
- **Morgan Stanley transference** — assuming GS patterns match MS patterns. Treat MS experience as hypotheses to verify, not rules to apply.
